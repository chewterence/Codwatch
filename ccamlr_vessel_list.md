# CCAMLR Authorized Vessel List — Dissostichus eleginoides Fleet

**Source:** https://www.ccamlr.org/en/compliance/list-authorised-vessels  
**Last fetched:** 2026-06-28  
**Maintained by:** Commission for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources (CCAMLR)  
**Purpose in this project:** Ground-truth list of every vessel legally permitted to fish Patagonian toothfish (*Dissostichus eleginoides*) in the Southern Ocean. Used to build GFW vessel ID lookup, screen suppliers, and flag unauthorized fishing activity.  
**Scope:** All vessels with any Dissostichus eleginoides or Dissostichus spp. authorization. Pure krill vessels (Euphausia superba only) are excluded.

---

## What this list means

- **CCAMLR** manages all fishing in the Southern Ocean (Convention Area). No vessel can legally catch toothfish there without appearing on this list.
- **Authorization period** — the window a vessel is licensed to fish. Fishing outside these dates = illegal.
- **Fishing area** — CCAMLR subareas/divisions. Each is a specific Southern Ocean zone with its own quota.
- **Target species in this list:**
  - *Dissostichus eleginoides* = Patagonian toothfish — your fish, caught mainly in 48.x and 58.x subareas
  - *Dissostichus spp.* = authorization covers both Patagonian and Antarctic toothfish — vessel may supply Dissostichus eleginoides
  - *Dissostichus mawsoni* = Antarctic toothfish — similar fish, predominantly caught in 88.x (Ross Sea). Vessels authorized for D. mawsoni only in 88.x are less likely to be your supplier.
- **Flag state** — the country whose laws govern the vessel. Not necessarily where the owner is based.
- **Excluded from this list:** Pure krill vessels (*Euphausia superba* only) — see excluded list at bottom.
- Any vessel fishing CCAMLR waters for Dissostichus eleginoides NOT on this list = **IUU (Illegal, Unreported, Unregulated) fishing**.

## How to use this list in the app

- **Supplier screening:** Search the supplier's vessel name here before purchasing. If absent → do not buy.
- **Priority suppliers to focus on:** Vessels with ✅ (48.x/58.x zones) are your most likely sources of Dissostichus eleginoides. The ⚠️ vessels fish predominantly for Dissostichus mawsoni in the Ross Sea — less likely to be your supplier unless they specifically declare D. eleginoides catch.
- **GFW lookup:** Search each vessel via `GET /vessels/search` to get the GFW vessel ID. GFW stores CCAMLR authorization under `registryPublicAuthorizations[].sourceCode = ["CCAMLR"]`.
- **Authorization expiry:** Compare today's date against authorization periods. Flag vessels at sea after expiry.
- **Area compliance:** Cross-reference GFW fishing event subareas against each vessel's authorized areas here. Fishing in a subarea not on their license = violation.
- **IUU cross-check:** Compare against the CCAMLR IUU vessel list at https://www.ccamlr.org/en/compliance/iuu-vessel-lists (20 vessels, PDF).

## Notes & Limitations

- Point-in-time snapshot (2026-06-28). CCAMLR updates seasonally — re-scrape each December (start of fishing season).
- Multiple authorization rows per vessel (different areas, overlapping periods) are consolidated above — check the CCAMLR source for full row-by-row detail.
- "Dissostichus spp." authorization legally covers both species. In practice, 88.x vessels primarily catch Dissostichus mawsoni — verify with the vessel's Catch Documentation Scheme (CDS) certificate, which accompanies every legal toothfish shipment.
- Flag state ≠ vessel owner country. Flag-of-convenience arrangements are common in this fleet.
- Authorization by CCAMLR does not guarantee fishing within quota — it only confirms the vessel is permitted in those waters. GFW event data is needed to monitor actual behavior.
